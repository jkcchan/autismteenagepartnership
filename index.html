About us

Initiative

Autism Teenage Partnership (ATP) is an initiative dedicated to providing accessible, free, and safe spaces for teens diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) to express themselves through games, crafts, and team-building activities. Individuals with ASD have deficits in communication and are at greater risk for social isolation and mental health issues due to declining services targeting their age range in the community. As students with siblings diagnosed with ASD, we understand this personal struggle and aim to support individuals with ASD and their families by delivering weekly social programming tailored to their unique needs.

Autism Teenage Partnership currently runs every Wednesday nights from 6-8pm at Milliken Park Community Recreation Centre, located at the south-east corner of McCowan Road and Steeles Avenue E.

History

The Autism Awareness initiative was started as a university club at Western University by Janson Chan and his best friend, Brandon Wildfong. The club became known as Autism Awareness at Western (AAW) and worked closely with Autism Ontario, London Chapter to host events such as Teen Night. Autism Awareness at Western (AAW) continues to run today with over 120 members supporting the surrounding community to raise autism awareness.

Due to the success of the program and its benefit to the London community, Janson was inspired to begin an autism awareness initiative in the GTA once he returned to began his studies at York University. He became involved with YorkU's Agent's of Change Program, which provided him with the initial tools and resources to establish an Autism Awareness program at York University (AAY).

We launched AAY as Autism Teenage Partnership in the summer of 2014 and have reached out to over 25 participants in the local GTA and neighbouring Markham area. Our program only continues to grow with widespread success and continued support networks for teens with ASD and their families.

Mission

Autism Teenage Partnership aims to positively impact the lives of individuals diagnosed with autism and their families through:

  1. Fostering the development of social skills and friendships to enhance the quality of life for our participants and allow for easy integration into society through creation of inclusive environments and strong social support networks.
  2. Work with local agencies in the surrounding community to develop relationships, raise awareness and understanding of Autism Spectrum Disorders and fight the associated stigma through educational initiatives.
  3. Develop toolkits incorporating our protocols and programming for other underserved regions in Ontario to initiate their very own ATP chapters in their local community.

Sponsors

  • York University - Faculty of Health

    Agents of Change: http://stong.yorku.ca/agents-of-change/

    The Agents of Change program associated with YorkU's Faculty of Health has provided the initial platform for ATP to launch with access to funding, blog websites, engagement opportunities, and media opportunities such as connections for a feature on Maclean's Magazine. Additionally, ATP collaborates with a mentor and advisor, Dr. Jonathan Weiss, who is a Clinical Psychologist and Associate Professor, and currently sits as the CIHR Chair in Autism Spectrum Disorders Treatment and Care Research at York University.

  • RNAO Region 7

    As nursing students we found it important to connect with our professional association, the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario (RNAO), who has provided funding and exposure through media releases and speaking engagements at regional and national conferences.

  • Milliken Park Advisory Council

    This council is a community association in the GTA associated with the community centre that hosts ATP. They have generously provided funding in-kind services, such as access to the venue, equipment, and exposure through media releases.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Where are you located?

    Autism Teenage Partnership is held at Milliken Park Community Recreation Centre which is located at the south-east corner of McCowan Road and Steeles Avenue. Address: 4325 McCowan Rd, Scarborough, ON M1V 4P1

  • When do you run your program?

    Our program is held year round every Wednesdays nights from 6pm to 8pm.

  • What are the current services you offer?

    Currently Autism Teenage Partnership offers weekly social programming for teens with ASD such as board games, crafts, and team-building activities co-ordinated by our program director. We are still in the process of working towards providing field trips and excursions.

  • What ages do you serve?

    Our participants vary in age from 14 years to 25 years old. However, Autism Teenage Partnership is open to individuals of all ages.

  • Do I have to pay for your program?

    No. Our program is provided to individuals and their families free of charge.

  • Do you have any volunteering opportunities?

    Yes. We collaborate with fellow university students to deliver our weekly social programming and engage with our participants. All volunteers are required to have a police check prior to beginning their volunteer position with us, which will be reimbursed. For further volunteer inquiries you may contact Brandon Wildfong at autsimteenagepartnership@gmail.com

  • How are you funded?

    Autism Teenage Partnership is largely funded by Milliken Park Advisory Council, the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario-Region 7 and York University.

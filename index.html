About us

Initiative

Autism Teenage Partnership (ATP) is an initiative dedicated to providing accessible, free, and safe spaces for teens diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). We support individuals with ASD by delivering weekly social programming that enables them to express themselves through games, crafts, and team-building activities.

ATP currently runs every Wednesday nights from 6-8pm at Milliken Park Community Recreation Centre, located at the south-east corner of McCowan Road and Steeles Avenue E.

History

The Autism Awareness initiative was started as a university club at Western University by Janson Chan and Brandon Wildfong. The club became known as Autism Awareness at Western (AAW) and continues to run with over 120 members raising autism awareness. When Janson returned to the GTA to study at YorkU, he became involved with their Agent’s of Change Program enabling him to establish Autism Awareness at York University (AAY).

AAY was launched as Autism Teenage Partnership in the summer of 2014 and has helped over 25 participants in the local GTA and neighbouring Markham area. Our program only continues to grow with widespread success and continued support for teens with ASD and their families.

Mission

Autism Teenage Partnership aims to positively impact the lives of individuals diagnosed with autism and their families through:

  1. Fostering the development of social skills and friendships to enhance quality of life and form social support networks.
  2. Allow for easy integration into society through the creation of inclusive and safe environments.
  3. Work with local agencies to develop relationships and raise awareness of Autism Spectrum Disorders.
  4. Develop toolkits for others to initiate ATP Chapters in underserved regions in Ontario.

Sponsors

  • Autism Ontario

    Autism Ontario provides ATP with promotion, exposure, volunteer training and outreach to potential participants and their families through mailing lists and e-newsletters.

  • York University - Faculty of Health

    Agents of Change: http://stong.yorku.ca/agents-of-change/

    YorkU’s Faculty of Health-Agents of Change Program has allowed ATP to launch with access to funding, a blogging website, engagement opportunities, and media opportunities such as a feature in Maclean’s Magazine. Additionally, Dr. Jonathan Weiss, a Clinical Psychologist and Associate Professor, who is the CIHR Chair in Autism Spectrum Disorders Treatment and Care Research at York University, serves as our mentor and advisor.

  • RNAO Region 7

    As nursing students we found it important to connect with our professional association, the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario (RNAO), who have provided funding and exposure through media releases and speaking engagements at regional and national conferences.

  • Milliken Park Advisory Council

    This council is a community association in the GTA associated with the community centre that hosts ATP. They have generously provided funding and in-kind services, such as access to the venue, equipment, and media releases.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Where are you located?

    Autism Teenage Partnership is held at Milliken Park Community Recreation Centre which is located at the south-east corner of McCowan Road and Steeles Avenue. Address: 4325 McCowan Rd, Scarborough, ON M1V 4P1

  • When do you run your program?

    Our program is held year round every Wednesdays nights from 6pm to 8pm.

  • What are the current services you offer?

    Currently Autism Teenage Partnership offers weekly social programming for teens with ASD such as board games, crafts, and team-building activities co-ordinated by our program director. We are still in the process of working towards providing field trips and excursions.

  • What ages do you serve?

    Our participants vary in age from 14 years to 25 years old. However, Autism Teenage Partnership is open to individuals of all ages.

  • Do I have to pay for your program?

    No. Our program is provided to individuals and their families free of charge.

  • Do you have any volunteering opportunities?

    Yes. We collaborate with fellow university students to deliver our weekly social programming and engage with our participants. All volunteers are required to have a police check prior to beginning their volunteer position with us, which will be reimbursed. For further volunteer inquiries you may contact Brandon Wildfong at autismteenagepartnership@gmail.com

  • How are you funded?

    Autism Teenage Partnership is largely funded by Milliken Park Advisory Council, the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario-Region 7 and York University.
